How We Calculate Your AI Visibility Score — The Full Breakdown

April 7, 2026  ·  6 min read  ·  by pickedby.ai

A user on Reddit left a comment after checking their score:

"I'd like to know more about how the score is actually calculated. I see in the FAQs you've briefly outlined some metrics used, but I'd like a more thorough explanation. Perhaps worth doing a blog post?"

Fair ask. Here's the full answer.

Why scoring AI visibility is different from Google rankings

Google rankings are relatively transparent. You can check your position for a keyword, track backlinks, measure page speed. The rules are documented.

AI visibility doesn't work that way.

When someone asks ChatGPT "what's the best Notion template for budgeting?" — there's no ranked list, no position 1 through 10. There's just: does your product get mentioned, or not?

We had to build a scoring system that reflects this reality. The result is a 0–100 score built from five dimensions, each measuring a different layer of how AI systems perceive and surface your product.

The 5 dimensions we measure

1
Direct name recognition

Does AI know your product exists when asked about it by name? This is the baseline. If you score zero here, you're invisible — AI has no record of you at all.

2
Best-of recommendation

When someone asks "what are the best tools for X?" — does your product make the list? Being known and being recommended are very different things. This is the hardest dimension to crack.

3
Category ranking

Does your product show up when AI lists tools in your category? "Free Gumroad alternatives", "best digital product platforms" — does your name appear in that context?

4
Comparison search

When someone asks AI to compare tools, does your product come up as a legitimate option? This requires being present enough in AI training data to be considered a peer to established names.

5
Reviews and external mentions

Has AI absorbed external content about your product — reviews, directory listings, community discussions? This is the external signal layer that feeds all the others.

What the scores actually look like

In the Reddit thread where we launched, three real scores told three very different stories.

0
Not found AI hasn't encountered this product in its training data yet. All five dimensions failing. This is a starting point, not a verdict — one solid external mention can move this within weeks.
40
Known but not recommended Direct name search and reviews passing. Best-of recommendations, category ranking, and comparison searches all failing. The most common pattern we see: AI knows you exist, but rarely recommends you.
82
Recommended All five dimensions passing or partially passing. The result of consistent work — structured content, external mentions, showing up in the right contexts over time.

The gap nobody talks about

The most important insight from building this tool:

Being known by AI and being recommended by AI are not the same thing.

Most creators assume that if they exist online, AI will find them and surface them. That's partially true — AI does index a lot. But AI doesn't recommend everything it knows. It recommends what it has seen described, compared, and discussed across multiple independent sources.

The bridge between those two states is external signal: other sources mentioning your product by name, in the context of recommendations. A directory listing. A Reddit thread. A comparison article. An honest review somewhere credible.

One or two of those can move a 40 to a 60. A handful of them, consistently, is how you reach 80+.

How scores change over time

AI systems update their training data. A product that scores 0 today can score 40 in a month if it gets listed in the right places. A product at 40 can reach 80 if it starts showing up in community discussions and comparison contexts.

This is why we built the dashboard — check once, make changes, come back and see if the number moved.

One of the users in our Reddit thread put it well: "I'll come back in a few weeks to try again and see if the score improves." That's exactly the right approach.
